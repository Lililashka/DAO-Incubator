Experiments

At DAOincubator we bring together builders, users, researchers and investors to the age of DAOs, channeling their zeal into experiments that yield real-world implementations. While this technology is still in its infancy, we’re working towards making DAOs into the complex organisms that they are capable to become.

We aim to provide individualized attention to our advisees and to maintain a field of critical research and practical knowledge that grows together with the rest of the space. We’re building a coordinated ecosystem of open collaboration, where people with a wide range of specializations and viewpoints can be tapped to help develop projects and ideas. By documenting our progress and our observations, we can also share key takeaways with the space as a whole.

Ecosystem

Decentralized Autonomous Organizations (DAOs) are a broad category of entities that live on blockchain networks. Well known DAO use cases include mining pools and decentralized exchanges, though there are lesser known DAOs that do everything from keeping stablecoins stable to rewarding memers.

The first DAO, known literally as TheDAO, went live in 2016 and quickly failed catastrophically. It was a lesson about the immense amount of care and deliberation that must go into the process of designing and building DAOs. Since then, several teams, notably Aragon, DAOstack and Colony, have been hard at work creating the tools for building DAOs, and several DAOs have managed to launch successfully.

The one thing missing from the picture was an environment where DAO builders can collaborate around attacking shared problems. Enter DAOincubator. We connect those creating DAOs with specialist advisors and help to distill and compile valuable insights about what works in decentralized governance. That is our contribution to this collective journey into the next chapter of DAO history.

We’re also working with several groups in the space to map out the decentralized governance landscape. Drop us a line if you want to participate!

About

We provide common resources and a support system to teams experimenting with blockchain-based governance structures. Our aim is to bolster the efforts of those building DAOs by offering research assistance and connecting them with specialists to discuss governance strategies, legal matters, and other crucial questions. More than anything else, we’re committed to pushing the blockchain space forward by nurturing teams whose projects are novel, socially or technologically impactful, and attainable with today’s tech.

DAOincubator began with just a couple of “cells” and organically grew into a larger symbiotic environment. Beginning with Cem Dagdalen and Daniel Shavit, our members pulled in people, who pulled in more people, until we grew to become the entity-of-many-complementary-parts that we are today. Having matured past the self-incubation phase, we’re now integrating orbital contributors, people who are involved in important areas of DAO research and have graciously agreed to engage when their insights are needed.

DAOincubator is a pool of knowledge and experience, built for the purpose of helping DAOs become the powerful, versatile organizations we believe they can be. We study the decentralized governance space to capture insights about useful models and best practices for running organizations on the blockchain and support teams that are using this experimental technology to create organizations. We also offer consulting services related to all things DAO. Come say hi.
